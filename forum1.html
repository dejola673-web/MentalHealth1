Need advice on supporting a loved one with anxiety? Professional guidance welcome

Hi everyone,

I'm reaching out because my partner has been struggling with severe anxiety for the past few months. I want to be supportive but sometimes I'm not sure what the right thing to say or do is. Does anyone have experience with this? Any tips from those who have been through similar situations would be really appreciated.

Some specific things I'm struggling with:

  • How do I respond when they're having a panic attack?
  • Should I encourage them to face their fears or give them space?
  • How can I take care of myself while supporting them?
  • When should I suggest professional help if they haven't already sought it?

I love them so much and just want to help in the best way possible. Thank you all for any advice you can share.

First of all, thank you for being such a caring partner. Your willingness to learn and support speaks volumes. Here are some evidence-based approaches:

During a panic attack: Stay calm and reassuring. Help them focus on breathing - try the 4-7-8 technique (inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8). Don't minimize their feelings or tell them to "just calm down." Instead, remind them that what they're feeling will pass.

Regarding facing fears vs. giving space: This depends on the situation. Professional exposure therapy is different from forcing someone into anxiety-provoking situations. Support their treatment plan if they have one.

Self-care is crucial: You can't pour from an empty cup. Make sure you're maintaining your own mental health, boundaries, and support system.

I have anxiety myself, so I can share what helps me when my partner supports me:

  • Ask what they need instead of assuming. Sometimes I need a hug, sometimes I need space.
  • Learn their triggers and warning signs together
  • Celebrate small victories with them
  • Don't take it personally if they cancel plans - anxiety is unpredictable
  • Encourage professional help but don't nag about it

And please, PLEASE take care of yourself too. You can't pour from an empty cup. Consider joining a support group for partners of people with anxiety.

Been there! My wife has dealt with anxiety for years. The best thing I did was go to a few therapy sessions with her (with her permission of course). The therapist gave me specific tools and helped me understand what was happening in her brain during anxious moments.

Also recommend the book "Loving Someone with Anxiety" by Kate N. Thieda. Really helped me understand how to be supportive without being overbearing.
